🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Checklist for Students
General Digital Hygiene
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) for your university portal, Gmail, and GitHub accounts instead of just SMS.

Secure Your Webcam and Mic: Use physical covers or software permissions to ensure your camera is only active when you intend it to be.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank or student portal using open campus or cafe Wi-Fi; use a VPN or mobile hotspot instead.

Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your contacts, location, and photos on your smartphone.

Backup Data Regularly: Keep your project files and research on an encrypted cloud service or an offline hard drive to mitigate the impact of Ransomware.

Check Link Authenticity: Hover over links in emails to see the actual URL before clicking, especially for "urgent" VIT administration or placement cell notices.

Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving: Remember that entering your UPI PIN is only required to send money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a refund" or "claim a prize," it is a scam.

Verify Merchant Names: Before hitting 'Pay' on apps like PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm, double-check the verified name of the recipient displayed on the screen to ensure it matches the intended person or store.
